Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Arkansas

I study how organisms interact with their surroundings (ecology) and how their DNA changes over space and time as a consequence (evolution). Ecology and evolution are often studied independently.

However, a deep understanding of how they work together is key to effectively conserving biodiversity.

My work focuses on bridging the gap between ecology and evolutionary biology via spatial ecology. I genotype thousands of individuals across all kinds of species to learn how ecology determines evolution and how their interaction should inform conservation.

While much of my work focuses on understanding the factors that govern fish diversity across riverscapes, I am fascinated by all biodiversity. Explore the site and check out my work!
